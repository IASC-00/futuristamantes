## Visualization Brief

This is the design brief for the compositor / visualization artist. Each location has a "before" state (live action) and a "transformed" state (composited). The brief describes the transformed state per location, with design language and constraints.

### Universal design principles

1. **Architectural realism.** Every transformation must look like it could be designed and built by a real architect. Respect rooflines, structural columns, materiality, scale.
2. **No sci-fi.** No floating panels, no glowing-blue holograms, no future-tech tropes. The reference language is *contemporary architecture and built-environment renderings* — think IDEO, Studio Gang, BIG, contemporary food-systems architecture.
3. **Light continues.** The compositor should match the lighting of the live action plate. Cold gray morning on the plate → cold gray morning on the transformed elements.
4. **Materiality.** Glass, steel, plant matter, warm wood, soft fabric. Not chrome, not neon, not plastic gloss.
5. **People belong.** Transformed spaces should include people — chefs harvesting, families dining, workers operating cultivation systems. Composite real people into the scene, scaled correctly.
6. **No charity coding.** Especially in the church sequence. Communal meals are framed as dignity and abundance, not as relief or service.

### Per-location transformation briefs

#### SOUTH Restaurant (Location 1)

- **Adds:** rooftop greenhouse structure (glass + light steel, fitting current building footprint), vertical hydroponic towers on side wall, edible wall as exterior accent, courtyard cultivation if there's adjacent space, chefs harvesting produce visible through windows
- **Keeps:** the building's existing facade, signage, street presence
- **People:** chefs in motion, diners visible inside
- **Tone:** elegant, restaurant-grade, NOT industrial

#### Honeysuckle (Location 2)

- **Adds:** rooftop farming structure, glass cultivation structures visible street-level, community greenhouse spaces adjacent, neighborhood gathering activity outside (people, market activity)
- **Keeps:** the storefront, the neighborhood texture
- **People:** neighborhood crowd, community gathering energy
- **Tone:** warm, community-coded

#### The Garage Building (Location 3)

- **Adds:** indoor vertical farming system filling the interior bays, robotics moving through racks, food science lab elements (clean glass panels, plant racks), workers in clean uniforms moving efficiently
- **Keeps:** the industrial exterior shell, the existing structural columns
- **People:** workers in motion (suggested 3–5 visible at once)
- **Tone:** clean, technical, industrial-but-precise. The transformation is "industrial reborn" — the building stays industrial in feel, but the activity inside is precision food production

#### The Church (Location 4)

- **Adds:** long communal tables filling the nave, families seated together eating fresh food, warm pendant lighting overhead, fresh-food platters on tables, stained glass casting color onto the scene
- **Keeps:** the church architecture, the stained glass, the structural beauty of the interior
- **People:** multi-generational families. Variety. Eating, talking, smiling. NOT serving lines, NOT plastic trays.
- **Tone:** dignity, beauty, community. The most important visualization to get right — the script's thesis line lands here ("Food is not charity. Food is infrastructure.")

#### Samuel Staten Building (Location 5)

- **Adds:** workforce training spaces (robotics labs, culinary incubation kitchens, greenhouse certifications signage, logistics command center with monitors), young Philadelphians learning, instructors guiding
- **Keeps:** the building's institutional character
- **People:** trainees and instructors, age-diverse, race-diverse (reflecting Philadelphia)
- **Tone:** active, focused, hopeful. The visual is "education that leads to work"

#### PCA Building (Location 6)

- **Adds:** packaging machines, sauce production lines, prepared meal assembly, microgreens production, mushrooms in racks, plant-protein production, prominent labels visible reading "GROWN IN PHILADELPHIA" and "EXPORTING FROM PHILADELPHIA"
- **Keeps:** the industrial footprint, loading dock, freight scale
- **People:** workers moving through the operation, plus visible products and labels
- **Tone:** real food production at scale — proud, clean, operational

#### North Broad Drone Finale (Location 7)

- **Adds:** the entire corridor transforms — greenhouses on rooftops, public markets at street level, plant-based cafes, artists painting murals, kids learning in visible courtyards, hydroponics towers, community kitchens, live music vignettes, public dining spaces
- **Keeps:** the corridor's spine, the city's recognizable skyline beyond
- **People:** street-level activity visible from drone height
- **Tone:** alive. The corridor glows green and the city is whole. This is the film's emotional peak; the visualization must deliver.

## Score Brief (for composer)

### Brief

A short documentary about Philadelphia's possible future. The film opens silent, in cold morning light. A man walks alone down North Broad Street. Then he asks: *what if the best version of Philadelphia hasn't happened yet?* And the music begins.

The score is the film's emotional climate — restrained, hopeful, civic. Not orchestral swell. Not corporate. Not minimalist-cold. The reference language is contemporary documentary score with serious civic weight: Jóhann Jóhannsson's *Last and First Men*, Hildur Guðnadóttir's *Joker* restraint (not the menace, the restraint), early Nils Frahm.

### Specifics

| Beat | Cue | Direction |
|---|---|---|
| Cold open | NO MUSIC | Sound design only — the city's own voice. 45–60 sec. |
| Title card lands | Cue 1 enters | Single sustained note. Builds slowly. Sparse piano + bowed strings. |
| Walk to SOUTH | Cue 1 continues | Sustained, doesn't pull focus from V.O. |
| Building transformations begin | Cue 2 | Adds shimmer, harmonic motion. Still restrained. Reverberant. |
| The Church | Cue 3 — drop | Music drops to near silence. One sustained note. Lets the visual carry. |
| "Food is not charity" line | Cue 3 swells slightly | Subtle re-emergence. Bowed strings. |
| Workforce + processing sequences | Cue 4 | Steady forward motion. Pulse without drum. |
| Drone finale | Cue 5 — swell | Music finally swells. Strings build, piano figures, a sense of arrival. |
| Final voice-over | Cue 5 continues to fade | Slow decay as voice-over closes. |
| Final card | Single note + silence | Lands the title. |

### Technical

- Stereo + 5.1 stems
- Tempo flexibility — score will be revised to picture lock; deliverables marked to final cut points
- All cues original composition. No needle-drop.
- Score length total: ~3.5–5 minutes (full film runtime is 4–6 min including the silent opening)
